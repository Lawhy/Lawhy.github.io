The **Graph Neural Network** (GNN) implements a function $$\tau(\mathcal{G}, n) \in \mathbb{R}^m$$ that maps a graph $$\mathcal{G}$$ and one of its node $$n$$ into an $$m$$-dimensional Euclidean space. The originally proposed GNN is an extension of both `recursive neural networks` and `random walk models`. GNNs are based on an `information diffusion mechanism` (propagating the information to neighbors) which is constrained to ensure that a <u>unique</u> <u>stable</u> `equilibrium` always exists.
